01 · POSITIONING

Brand Voice & Positioning

"What if they're not people leaving unlawful lifestyles... but entrepreneurs?"
"Most systems try to fix behavior. That's not the problem. The behavior isn't the issue. It's where it's being applied."

The whole site is built around one shift: your audience already knows how to run something. They've taken risks, managed people, moved money under pressure. The system wasn't the problem with what they did. The law was.

That single move changes how the site sounds. Not "we'll help you become a better person" — the goal isn't a different person, it's the same person doing legal work. The voice should be:

  • Direct. No charity language. No "journey of healing." Talk to them like adults who already know how the world works.
  • Honest. Don't oversell. Don't promise. Don't motivate.
  • Specific. Use real verbs. "Ran something," "moved money," "managed people" — not "redirect their drive."

02 · VISUAL DIRECTION

Visual Direction

Color Palette

#023D69 — Primary Blue
#F9870B — Accent Orange

Typography

Headers: Libre Franklin Black/Bold

Body: Tahoma or Aptos

Real people. Real settings. No "second chance" stock imagery — no broken chains, no sunrise photos, no hands reaching out. Photos of actual participants and mentors when possible. The visual tone matches the voice: serious, professional, no theatrics.

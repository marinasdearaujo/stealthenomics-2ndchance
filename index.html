01 · POSITIONING

Brand Voice & Positioning

"What if you're not a lifelong criminal... but a lifelong entrepreneur?"
"Most systems try to fix behavior. That's not the problem. The behavior isn't the issue. It's where it's being applied."

The whole site is built around one shift: you already know how to run something. You've taken risks, managed people, moved money under pressure. The system wasn't the problem with what you did. The law was.

What you don't have — what nobody's shown you — is the path out. The path that ends in a real business, not another sentence.

Voice Rules

  • "You" not "they." Direct address. The audience is reading and recognizing themselves.
  • "Equip" not "help." Empowering, not patronizing. They're capable adults — we give them tools, we don't rescue them.
  • "Pathway" framing. The thing they're missing is the path, not the drive. We provide the path.
  • "Street entrepreneur" — the audience descriptor. They were entrepreneurs in a different market.
  • Direct. Honest. Specific. No charity language, no journeys of healing, no overselling.

02 · TWO AUDIENCES

Two Audiences Strategy

The site speaks to two audiences with different needs. Here's how the home page handles both without losing focus:

Primary: Street Entrepreneurs

Men and women with a history of Unlawful Entrepreneurship™ who want to redirect their skills.

What they see: Most of the home page. The story is theirs. The hero, the problem, the solution, the pathway, the testimonials — it all centers on them.

Primary CTA: Register for the Info Session

Secondary: Mentors & Contributors

People with experience or resources who want to give back. Mentors give time. Contributors fund the program.

What they see: The full entrepreneur story first (so the mission is clear), then a dedicated section at the bottom of the home page asking them to get involved.

Primary CTA: "I want to get involved" → links to /get-involved page (P1, post-launch)

Why this works: Mentors and donors are persuaded by impact, not by being targeted. When they see the entrepreneur's story first — really feel the mission — the ask hits differently. This is how charity:water, Defy Ventures, and most successful impact nonprofits structure their sites.

The Contact form already routes mentor inquiries via the "What brings you here?" dropdown.

03 · VISUAL DIRECTION

Visual Direction

Color Palette

#023D69 — Primary Blue
#F9870B — Accent Orange

Typography

Headers: Libre Franklin Black/Bold

Body: Tahoma or Aptos

Real people. Real settings. No "second chance" stock imagery — no broken chains, no sunrise photos, no hands reaching out. Photos of actual participants and mentors when possible. The visual tone matches the voice: serious, professional, no theatrics.
